Creating a self-bootstrapping document classification method

So here is a more detailed version of our project

Application

  • News Aggregation: In our project, we will automatically categorize financial news articles, and this will enable users to easily access the most relevant content without having to go through an overwhelming amount of information. This capability will keep users abreast of market trends and significant events.

  • Investment Research: We intend to create a system that classifies research papers and investment reports in a way that analysts can retrieve easily the studies of interest to them or reflective of the current market situation. This will help this domain to make research more efficient and facilitate informed decision-making.

  • Content Management Systems: Our approach will be carried out in content management systems to manage large volumes of financial content on websites and databases. In this way, we can enhance the user experience through better navigation for readers.

Risk Management: We are aware that articles that might affect risk considerations need to be classified. Our project will help find any eventual risk or opportunity to which an organization can respond by studying classified articles in order to facilitate strategic decision-making and subsequent risk handling.

Implementation

Data collection We shall first collect a set of financial articles from news and research-based sources, academic journals, and financial blogs. We are now going to obtain this massive amount of data which would let the model learn the largest possible variety of topics and writing styles.

  • Text Preprocessing : After the loading step we would then get the clean text data preprocessed for this stage. This process shall include the removal of stopwords stemming, and general text normalization techniques for readiness in the analysis. This stage is very fundamental and contributes much to improved quality and accuracy in ensuring a good classification model.

  • Initial Classification: We will develop an initial classification model using supervised learning from a labeled dataset. We will use this model as a base to classify articles into predefined financial topics.

  • Self-Bootstrapping:
    We will implement a self-bootstrapping mechanism where the model classifies the unlabeled articles. The classifications will be validated by human feedback at regular intervals for accuracy and relevance.
    With that self-feedback loop, we could retrain this periodically enhancing and adapting to the ever-emerging financial terminology and the trends over time.

  • Deployment: Our refined classification model will then be deployed in a comfortable applicative platform. This process lets the real-time categorize the articles, which implies the user can get accessed categorized articles instantly.

Scalability

  • Modular Architecture: We design our system in modular architecture so that every component of it, such as data collection, processing, and classification, can be scaled independently. This approach gives us flexibility and accommodates future growth in user demand.

    We plan to use cloud infrastructure, because we will have managed lots of data and its connected computational workload for our model. It will supply the scale up required strength as our base user count grows.

  • Self-learning: This self-bootstrapping mechanism will assure that the model never stops learning due to the fact that it keeps updating and assimilating financial trends. This means that the classification system we are developing will be relevant and effective with time.
